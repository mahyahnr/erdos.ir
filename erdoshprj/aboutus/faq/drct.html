DrCT Creativity Olympiad

1. What kind of competition is DrCT?

The DrCT International Olympiad, like the Asian Mathematics and Science Olympiad, is organized by the Singapore International Olympiad Committee (SIMCC) in 21 Asian countries. In this Olympiad, very interesting and instructive issues of creativity and computational thinking are raised. To answer these questions, not much information is needed and the questions are riddle-like and aimed at evaluating the intelligence, creativity and way of thinking of the students. Solving such questions prepares the student's mind to design computer programs, and in this sense, this Olympiad is a very suitable background for students' success in the Computer Olympiad.

2. DrCT competitions are being held in Iran since what year?

In 2020, Iran joined these competitions as the 21st Asian country. We hope that we have been able to provide a bed so that school students in different regions of the country can learn informatics in a more creative way and compete with students in different parts of the world. The use of computational thinking gives students the opportunity to have a systematic approach to learning programming, and with the combination of design thinking and robotics, they have the necessary introduction to programming and doing more advanced projects using an electronic platform and a high standard. and be able to provide new methods in performing computing projects in the future.

3. What is the purpose of DrCT Olympiad?

The purpose of holding DrCT competitions is to foster creativity, logical reasoning ability, algorithmic thinking skills and computational thinking skills in students. By participating in this competition, students will gain a deeper understanding of the world around them with the design thinking method to create a robotic solution to the problem, which will enable them to solve problems using the power of computers and information technology. solve the everyday. Programming details and focusing on the solution does not only include writing a piece of code, but also includes useful algorithmic techniques and problem-solving skills that must be chosen correctly in order to achieve the desired result.

4. Who can participate in this competition?

All students from first to twelfth grade can participate in these competitions

5. When is the date of the competition?

The first stage of this competition is held every year in April.

6. What resources should be studied to prepare for these competitions?

The lesson plan of the competition and sample questions of 2020 are available on the site, in the competition section of DrCT. The bank of various questions in the field of Bebras can also be used as a useful educational resource for this exam. Interested students can use the samples of Bebras questions published by Fatemi Publications in the form of a book of Bebras riddles for preparation.

7. What is the level of DrCT competition questions?

The questions are presented in two sections: informatics questions and calculation questions, the level of the questions is at the level of introductory computer science topics.

8. How can I get a sample of questions from the previous years of Doctor CT competitions?

The lesson plan for the competition and sample questions for 2020 are available on www.erdos.ir and in the DrCT competition section

9. What are the benefits of participating in the DrCT competition?

All students who can win gold, silver and bronze medals in the first stage will receive the official plaque of the competition, and also the gold, silver and bronze medal winners will be allowed to participate in the second stage of the competition, which is held every year in an Asian country. they earn The best students from every level of education in grades 1 to 12 who can perform well in the first stage of the competition and win a gold or silver medal in the second stage or by participating in other Olympiads such as SASMO (mathematics) and VANDA (science) ) win the gold or silver medal of these competitions in the final stage, they can become a member of the academy of the best students of Asia and benefit from its advantages, which include various educational scholarships and cash gifts.

10. What is the number of questions in the DrCT competition and how to answer them?

The number of questions of the first stage is between 18 and 24 questions depending on the educational level, the number of points and how to answer them are explained in the competition brochure.

11. Do the questions have a negative score?

Yes, the questions have negative marks and the use of calculators is not allowed.

12. Do they have Persian translation?

Yes, the questions have Persian translation.

13. State the level of knowledge of the English language to participate in this competition?

In the first stage, you only need to know numbers and write numbers in English, and you don't need anything else.

14. How will the results of the competition be evaluated and announced?

The results of the first stage will be announced about 45 days after the competition. Gold medals will be awarded to the first 8% in Asia, silver medals to the next 12% in Asia, and bronze medals to the next 20% in Asia. 40% of all participants in Asia will be given medals in the first stage.

15. How many stages are DrCT competitions and where are they held?

This Olympiad has two stages, the first stage of which is held in Iran and the final stage, was held every year in an Asian country before Corona. Due to the spread of Corona, the final stage is currently held online in Iran.

16. Who receives gifts from Erdos Institute?

Every year, Erdos Institute gives valuable prizes to the first to third placers of the country in different levels, and to all the medalists of the final stage of the competitions and the best of all competitions in the fields of mathematics and sciences and DrCT, cash and non-cash prizes along with cups through Erdos Institute is awarded.

17. When and how will the preparation classes for DrCT competitions be held?

For more information, refer to the Erdos Institute’s Courses, Classes and Workshops page in the FAQ section.

18. How much is the registration fee for the first and second stage of the competition?

The registration fee for the first stage is about 10 dollars, which is announced every year in December depending on the dollar rate. The cost of participating in the second stage of the competition will be around 110 dollars.

19. How can we find out about the validity of these competitions?

After several years of successful establishment in the field of science olympiad, every year the number of countries joining this competition is increasing. Also, to ensure the validity and correctness of the documents handed over to the students, each board has a code that is only for that student and by scanning the code on the official website of the name and information competitions. The student's medal type can be checked.
